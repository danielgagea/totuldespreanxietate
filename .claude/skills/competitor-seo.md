# Competitor SEO

SEO competitive analysis. This skill identifies keyword gaps, content opportunities, backlink advantages, SERP feature ownership, and structural differences between your search presence and your competitors' to find the fastest paths to ranking improvement.

## When to Use This Skill

- Analyzing why competitors outrank you
- Finding keyword gaps (they rank, you don't)
- Identifying content topics competitors cover that you don't
- Planning content strategy based on competitive landscape
- Discovering SERP features you could capture
- Evaluating whether you can realistically compete for specific keywords

## Competitive Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Identify Your Real Competitors

Your SEO competitors may differ from your business competitors. SEO competitors are whoever ranks for the keywords you want.

**How to find them:**
1. Search your top 10 target keywords
2. Note which domains appear repeatedly in positions 1-10
3. These are your SEO competitors (even if they're not direct business competitors)

**Competitor types:**

| Type | Description | Strategic Approach |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Direct competitors | Same product/service, same audience | Study their entire strategy, find gaps |
| Content competitors | Rank for your keywords with informational content | Match or exceed content quality |
| Aggregator competitors | Directories, review sites, marketplaces | Hard to outrank directly, target long-tail instead |
| Giant competitors | Wikipedia, major publications, tech giants | Don't compete head-on, find specific niches |

### Step 2: Keyword Gap Analysis

**What it reveals:** Keywords competitors rank for that you don't.

**How to analyze (with data):**
If you have SEO tool data (Ahrefs, Semrush, etc.):
1. Pull competitor's ranking keywords
2. Pull your ranking keywords
3. Filter for keywords where they rank and you don't
4. Sort by search volume and relevance

**How to analyze (without tools):**
1. Review competitor's site structure (all pages, blog categories)
2. Note topics they cover that you don't
3. Search those topics and see where they rank
4. Check if those topics are relevant to your business

**Categorize gaps:**

| Gap Type | Description | Priority |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Easy wins | Low competition, you have related content already | High - create or expand content |
| Strategic gaps | Important keywords, competitors are weak | High - invest in quality content |
| Difficult gaps | High competition, competitor has strong content | Medium - only if keyword is vital |
| Not worth pursuing | Irrelevant or dominated by giants | Low - skip |

### Step 3: Content Gap Analysis

Beyond individual keywords, look at topic areas and content formats.

**Topic analysis:**
- What broad topics do competitors cover that you don't?
- What subtopics within shared topics are they covering more deeply?
- What content formats are they using that you're not? (Guides, tools, comparisons, glossaries)

**Content quality comparison:**

| Factor | How to Compare |
|--------|---------------|
| Depth | Word count and subtopic coverage vs. ranking competitors |
| Freshness | Last updated date vs. competitors |
| Original value | Unique data, examples, or analysis vs. generic content |
| Media | Images, videos, tools, calculators vs. text-only |
| E-E-A-T signals | Author credentials, citations, experience markers |

### Step 4: SERP Feature Analysis

SERP features (featured snippets, People Also Ask, image packs, etc.) are high-value real estate.

**Key SERP features to track:**

| Feature | What It Is | How to Win It |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| Featured snippet | Answer box above position 1 | Direct answer in first paragraph, use the exact question as header |
| People Also Ask | Expandable question boxes | Answer related questions clearly within content |
| Image pack | Row of images in results | Optimized images with descriptive alt text and file names |
| Video carousel | Video results | YouTube or embedded video with schema |
| Knowledge panel | Entity information box | Schema markup, Wikipedia presence, consistent entity info |
| FAQ rich result | Expandable FAQ below listing | FAQ schema markup on page |
| Sitelinks | Sub-page links below main result | Clear site structure, descriptive page titles |

**Feature ownership mapping:**

For your top 10 target keywords, document:

```
KEYWORD: _______________

Featured snippet: [Competitor URL or "None"]
People Also Ask: [Questions listed]
Image pack: [Present? Who owns it?]
Video results: [Present? Who owns them?]
FAQ results: [Who has them?]

OPPORTUNITY: [Can you capture any of these?]
```

### Step 5: Backlink Profile Comparison

Backlinks remain a ranking factor. Understanding competitor link profiles reveals why they rank and what you'd need to compete.

**What to compare:**
- Total referring domains (theirs vs. yours)
- Quality of linking domains (high-authority vs. low-quality)
- Link velocity (are they gaining links faster?)
- Link types (editorial links, directories, guest posts, etc.)

**Realistic assessment:**
If a competitor has 500 referring domains and you have 50, you won't outrank them on competitive keywords through content alone. Either:
- Target less competitive keywords where domain authority matters less
- Build a link acquisition strategy alongside content
- Compete on content depth and freshness for long-tail keywords

### Step 6: Strategic Advantage Assessment

After analyzing gaps, identify your actual competitive advantages:

| Advantage Type | Example | How to Leverage |
|---------------|---------|-----------------|
| Unique data | Proprietary research, first-party data | Create data-driven content competitors can't replicate |
| Niche expertise | Deep knowledge in specific subtopic | Go deeper than generalist competitors |
| Speed | Faster publishing on trending topics | Cover emerging topics before competitors |
| Format | Interactive tools, calculators | Build utility competitors don't offer |
| Experience | Real-world use of products/services | First-person reviews and case studies |

### Content Strategy Patterns

Identify what pattern competitors follow:

| Pattern | Description | Your Opportunity |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Volume play | Many thin articles | Beat with fewer, deeper articles |
| Depth play | Long, comprehensive guides | Match depth, add unique angle |
| Tool play | Free calculators, templates | Build better tools |
| Data play | Original research, surveys | Create your own research |
| Community play | Comments, forums, UGC | Build community features |
| Update play | Frequently refreshed content | Commit to regular updates |

## Red Flags

Issues that commonly indicate flawed competitive strategy:

- [ ] Targeting identical keywords as competitors with 10x your domain authority
- [ ] Copying competitor's content structure without understanding their link advantage
- [ ] Ignoring long-tail keywords where you can actually rank
- [ ] No awareness of which SERP features competitors own
- [ ] Competing on content volume against teams with 10x your resources
- [ ] Analyzing competitors without assessing your own strengths
- [ ] Assuming competitor strategy works for them (it might not; they might rank despite bad content due to authority)
- [ ] Chasing every gap instead of prioritizing by impact and achievability
- [ ] Not accounting for domain authority differences in keyword targeting
- [ ] Treating all competitors as equal threats
